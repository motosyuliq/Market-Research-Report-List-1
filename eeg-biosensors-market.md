<p><p>The EEG biosensors market can be categorized into three primary types: wired, wireless, and electrode connection systems. Wired EEG biosensors involve direct connections to a recording device, providing reliable data transmission. Wireless EEG sensors offer enhanced mobility and user convenience, as they transmit data without cables, enabling more natural movement during monitoring. Electrode connection systems focus on the type and arrangement of electrodes used to capture brain activity, influencing signal quality and user comfort. Each type serves distinct applications in research and clinical settings.</p></p>
