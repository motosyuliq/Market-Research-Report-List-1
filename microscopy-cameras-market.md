Monochrome microscopy cameras are designed to capture high-resolution images in grayscale, making them ideal for applications requiring enhanced sensitivity and contrast, such as fluorescence microscopy. Conversely, color microscopy cameras capture images in full color, allowing for a more detailed representation of the sample's features and structures. Each type serves distinct applications, catering to the varied needs of researchers and professionals in fields like biology, material science, and pathology.</p></p>
