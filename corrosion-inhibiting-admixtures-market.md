<p><p>Corrosion inhibiting admixtures are essential additives used in concrete to enhance durability and resistance to environmental factors. The market can be categorized into two main types: organic and inorganic. </p><p>Organic type admixtures typically consist of organic compounds that form a protective film on the steel reinforcement, reducing corrosion rates. In contrast, inorganic type admixtures include materials like calcium nitrate or lithium-based compounds that actively interact with the surrounding environment to inhibit corrosion. Both types are crucial in extending the lifespan of concrete structures.</p></p>
