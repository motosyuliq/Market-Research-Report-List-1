<p><p>Consumer finance can be categorized into two primary market types: unsecured and secured consumer finance. Unsecured consumer finance involves loans that are not backed by collateral, such as personal loans or credit cards, typically carrying higher interest rates due to the greater risk for lenders. In contrast, secured consumer finance is backed by collateral, like a car loan or mortgage, allowing borrowers to access larger amounts at lower interest rates since the lender has a guarantee for repayment through the asset involved.</p></p>
